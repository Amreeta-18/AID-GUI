GenderMag Automated Inclusivity Detector

What is The GenderMag Automated Inclusivity Detector?





This tool attempts to emulate a GenderMag software evaluation session and takes as input the data required when performing such a session, and checks for three decision rules.
The three decision rules are detailed below:

Rule 1: Cues to needed information

The first rule in our set checks for situations where users like Abi would not find all the information they need to complete their task. For example, if Abi has the subgoal of "Beginning the course", the wording of the subgoal serves as the information that Abi seeks before they proceed to the action, as we can see in the left snapshot below.

Rule 2: Situating Abi in the context of the action performed

On clicking a link, the destination page should offer cues to help Abi understand that they have reached the right place. If a webpage fails to use words similar to what a link's description hinted at, users like Abi could get confused.

Rule 3: Information link navigation

Users like Abi click on a link only after gathering enough information and planning their next step. Labeled and descriptive links provide Abi with information about the webpage they are supposed to visit. Rule 3 captures this.

More information about GenderMag can be found on our website. (Read our paper here: AID: An Automated Detector for Gender-Inclusivity Bugs in OSS Project Pages.)
