What is The GenderMag Automated Inclusivity Detector?

This tool automates the GenderMag method partially. The GenderMag Method (short for Gender Inclusiveness Magnifier) is a method for finding gender-inclusiveness issues in software features. Using GenderMag, software developers and/or user experience (UX) teams can evaluate their software from a gender-inclusiveness perspective. Fixing issues that may disproportionately affect one gender over another can lead to better usability for everyone.

More information about GenderMag can be found on our website. (Read our paper here: AID: An Automated Detector for Gender-Inclusivity Bugs in OSS Project Pages.)
